Claude's feature ecosystem is fracturing under its own complexity. Simultaneously, Hacker News is debating whether Claude Code itself is a "misfeature" (140 points, 118 comments), and users report Fable disappearing behind a credit paywall. For consultants selling Claude adoption, this is a critical inflection: your pitch just got harder because the tool itself is splintering into a pro/freemium divide with higher skill floors.

## What People Are Saying
- Boris Cherny emphasizes that Claude automation success isn't about single features but "using the right features with the right guardrails"—acknowledging that most users probably aren't there yet. The implication: there's a widening gap between "casual Claude users" and "teams that can trust the output."
- Hacker News users are actively questioning whether AI has broken Stack Overflow (69 points, 80 comments on "What AI did to stackoverflow in a graph"), suggesting growing concern that LLM-generated code is polluting the knowledge commons that Claude itself was trained on—a recursive credibility problem.
